Prague Architecture

Modern Architecture

Built during the early 1990s, the dancing house brought much attention because of its nonconformist design. The building was designed by architects Vlado Milunic and Frank O. Gehry, whose works include the Guggenheim Museum and the Walt Disney Concert Hall. The design of the building is reminiscent to a couple dancing. Hence, it was dubbed as the 'dancing house' or Ginger and Fred, after the famous dancers Ginger Rogers and Fred Astair. The male is represented by a rock tower and the woman is represented by a glass one. The building is now used as an office, a hotel, a gallery, and a restaurant.
