Municipal House

While it now serves as the grandest concert hall in Prague, the Municipal house originally stood where the Royal Court Palace was located. It was also the former residence of the King of Bohemia. The palace was demolished and construction on the Municipal House began in 1905. Designed by Antonin Balsanek and Osvald Polivka, the house is styled in a mix of Neobaroque and Art Nouveau styles. Many other artists collaborated to work on the building including Alfons Mucha, Ladislow Saloun, and Karel Pilar. The Municipal House is also of great importance to the Czech Republic as it was where the Independece of the Czechoslovakian Republic was announced. Today, it is a concert hall that houses more than 1,200 seats.
